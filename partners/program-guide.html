Google Cloud Platform Partner Program

The Google Cloud Platform Partner Program provides a rich set of training, technical, support, marketing, and sales resources for technology and services partners to help you build successful businesses that leverage our Cloud Platform offerings.

Google Cloud Platform Program Guide

I. Program Overview:

Program purpose: The purpose of the Google Cloud Platform Program is to enable participants to build their practices or products on the Google Cloud Platform, and to support and recognize participants as they reach higher levels of success.

Program guide: Participation in this Program is governed by the terms of the Cloud Platform Program Agreement between the parties and this Google Cloud Platform Program Guide (“Program Guide”). The Program Guide details the benefits and requirements associated with each level of the Program. Participation in the Program does not create any agency, partnership or joint venture between the participant and Google.

Participant types: The Program is designed for two participant types: Services participants and Technology participants:

  • Services participants provide consulting and implementation services on various Google Cloud Platform products. Clients can use Services participants’ knowledge and expertise to create applications and implement solutions for a variety of use cases.
  • Technology participants either provide tools which integrate with the Google Cloud Platform to extend its reach and functionality, or use one of the Google Cloud Platform products as a foundation for their products.

Each participant type has three levels - Registered Company, Authorized Partner and Premier Partner- as described below.

If you have any questions regarding the content of the Program Guide, please contact a Google partner manager.

IA. Services Participants:

  1. Generally: The Program enables new and existing Services participants to develop competencies to sell, build, deliver, support and maintain applications and implement solutions for clients on Google’s Cloud Platform products. Services participants include systems integrators, IT consulting firms, application developers, and other professional services and IT solution providers.
  2. Program Levels. There are three levels to the Program (collectively, “Levels”) for Services participants:
    1. Registered Company: This Level includes companies that have completed a Google Cloud Platform Program application, have satisfied the Registered Company and any other then-current requirements, and have executed a Google Cloud Platform Program Agreement.
    2. Authorized Services Partner: This Level includes companies that have executed a Google Cloud Platform Program Agreement, have completed an Authorized Services Partner application, have satisfied the Authorized Services Partner and any other then-current requirements, and have been formally accepted by Google, in Google’s sole discretion, as an Authorized Services Partner. Google will provide partners with notification of acceptance to the Program/Level, which notification may be by e-mail or by other appropriate means. These companies will also be given the opportunity to execute, a Google Cloud Platform Reseller Agreement. These companies may refer to themselves as a “Google Cloud Platform Authorized Services Partner.”
    3. Premier Services Partner: This Level is reserved for companies that have executed a Google Cloud Platform Program Agreement, have completed a Premier Services Partner application, and have satisfied the Premier Services Partner and any other then-current requirements. These companies will also be given the opportunity to execute, in their sole discretion, a Google Cloud Platform Reseller Agreement, if they have not done so already. These companies may refer to themselves as a “Google Cloud Platform Premier Services Partner.”
    4. For purposes of clarification, advancement from Registered Company to Authorized Services Partner is by invitation only and at Google’s sole discretion, based on participant’s application and qualifications. Once a company has become an Authorized Partner, however, advancement from Authorized Services Partner to Premier Services Partner will be granted automatically if the company has met the Premier Services Partner requirements.
  3. Program Benefits and Requirements. The requirements for participation in the Program as a Google Cloud Platform Registered Company, Authorized Services Partner, or Premier Services Partner, as well as the benefits of membership in a given level, are set forth in the tables below and the remainder of this Program Guide.

II. Other Information and Parameters

  1. Eligible Products. Only the Google Cloud Platform products listed here, or at another location to be specified by Google, are included as part of this Program Guide and are associated with the requirements and benefits of the Google Cloud Platform Program.
  2. Updating the Program Guide. This Program Guide may be updated periodically by Google. Google will update the Program Guide by placing the updated Program Guide at the Google Partner Connect web portalor another location to be specified. Program participants should periodically review the Program Guide during its participation in the Program to ensure its compliance with any updates.
  3. Annual Evaluation. Google will evaluate Program participants at the beginning of each year to ensure that they continue to meet the requirements for their specific level in the Program. If Google determines that a participant does not meet the requirements for their level, then Google may transition that participant out of the specific level, or out of the Program entirely. In the latter case, participants must cease usage of any logos and branding associated with the given program level, for which the participant previously qualified, immediately upon Google ’s notification to participant. By participating in the Program, participants acknowledge and agree that their failure to meet the minimum requirements under the Program, or using unethical business practices, may result in Google’s termination of a participant’s participation in the Program.
  4. Additional Definitions. Capitalized terms in this Program Guide are defined below. Any capitalized terms not defined below otherwise have the meaning given to them in the remainder of this Program Guide and the Cloud Platform Program Agreement.

“Annual Revenue Requirement”means the total amount of annual revenue from Partner-Resale Revenue, Partner-Led Revenue, and Partner-Assisted Revenue, which will be agreed upon by Google and participant via a partner-driven annual business plan, that participant will target for a given year.

“Deal” is a unique business transaction with a new or existing customer that includes a Google direct contract or a reseller contract.

“Google Partner Connect (Partner Resource Site)” means the site provided by Google through which participants can obtain relevant information regarding Google’s Cloud Platform Programs, as communicated by Google to Program participants from time to time. The location Google uses to provide information to participants may change at Google's sole discretion.

“Google Partner Program Logo and Branding” is how participants are permitted to refer to themselves when referencing their participation in the Google Cloud Platform Program. The branding guidelines can be found here.

“Partner-Resale Revenue” means the amount of revenue to Google from partner-resold end customer projects on the Google Cloud Platform.

“Partner-Led Revenue” means the a mount of revenue to Google from Google-sold end customer projects on the Google Cloud Platform for which the partner has led or significantly influenced the commercial aspects of the Deal. The Google partner manager will need to approve any revenue categorization as Partner-Led for a nominated Deal.

“Partner-Assisted Revenue” means the amount of revenue to Google from Google-sold end customer projects on the Google Cloud Platform for which the partner has provided services to assist in the commercial aspects of the Deal. The Google partner manager will need to approve any revenue categorization as Partner-Assisted for a nominated Deal.

“Published Customer Success Stories” means a case study regarding a successfully delivered customer project that involved either partner services work or partner complementary products on the Google Cloud Platform products, including then - current required content by Google, and following the format communicated by Google to Partners.
